--- Day 2: Chocolate Coins ---

While you are organizing your star coin collections, you accidentally spilt all of your coins, including some fake chocolate coins!

The chocolate coins look identical to the star coins, so there's no easy way to tell them apart. Fortunately, each coin is labeled with a unique ID, so you can use them to identify fake coins and separate them from your real star coins.

After picking up all the coins, you made a list of coin IDs (your puzzle input). You faintly remembered that the ID of star coins must not end with 0. That means that all coins whose ID ends with 0 must be a fake chocolate coin.

For example, consider the following list:

312
911
508
707
100

The first four coin IDs do not end with a 0, so those are valid star coin IDs. The final coin ID (100) ends with a 0, so it is not a valid coin ID, and thus is a fake chocolate coin.

To calculate the total value of star coins, exclude all invalid coin IDs and sum up all valid coin IDs. In the example above, the total value of star coins is 2438.
